Introduction#

Earlier this year, while looking for series to watch (particularly detective related), I stumbled upon a series called Galileo. The synopsis seemed quite interesting, so I decided to give it a try.

Galileo is a JDrama about a physics professor at Teito University who begrudgingly happens to help the Tokyo Metropolitan PD solve murder cases. usually, he is initially not interested, but in order to prove the scientific reasoning behind some mystery in the case (be it an alibi or the way the crime was commited) ends up getting involved in it.

The series is based on a series of books by Higashino Keigo, but while the cases (and specially the movies) may be inspired by them, the series has several adaptation-only elements in it, including who the detectives involved with professor Yukawa are.

Usually I get my K/JDrama from Nyaa, and while it did have torrents for this series and its movies, a lot of them were almost dead and had no seeders. Thankfully some very kind strangers over in The Netherlands and Australia seemed to be seeding the torrents at the time so I was able to download everything in the end.

Please note that I haven’t really ever reviewed something, so this might be a mess, but I consider it my favorite media I have consumed this year so I feel the need to write a bit about it. I will avoid talking about the episodes and the plot to avoid spoilers, and also cause I want this to be a review of my experience with the series overall and not a episode play-by-play.

Season 1 (2007)#

Here is where we see the biggest change between the books and the adaptation: While detective Kusanagi Shunpei (Kitamura Kazuki) is the main detective in the books, here he actually is just a friend who has worked with professor Yukawa in the past, and is now getting promoted and therefore introduces his succesor, detective Utsumi Kaoru (Shibasaki Kou), to professor Yukawa Manabu (Fukuyama Masaharu) to solve the new case.

My favorite moment in this entire series is when professor Yukawa realizes what the solution to the mystery of the case is and starts scribbling formulas wherever possible while this amazing banger plays in the background. While it started kinda cheesy, by the end of this series it had grown immensely on me, specially when characters start adressing it in some ways.

Overall, as an introduction to the universe and the characters, it works amazingly well.

Despite what may seem as romantic development (and I do agree that there was some tension at the time), I really enjoyed that the relationship between Yukawa and Utsumi grows to be more of really good and close friends.

Every actor absolutely kills it, but if there’s one that shines among all, it’s definitely Fukuyama-san. Even side characters like professor Kuribayashi (Watanabe Ikkei) and, ironically, detective Kusanagi, are involved enough in the story you get to enjoy their performances. It’s a shame other characters like Yukawa’s students and the forensic pathologists will get changed for Season 2.

Movie 0 - Galileo Zero (2008)#

This is a prequel to the entire Galileo series, set during the time Kusanagi was still working alongside Yukawa. The movie both depicts the first case they worked together on as well as the case in which both of them met, when Kusanagi was accused of commiting a murder, and how their friendship started.

The movie also includes a teaser to the next movie in the form of Yukawa talking to another student who’s attempting a different solution to the four color theorem.

A bit unrelated, but I actually watched this movie at the very last, after watching everything else.

Movie 1 - The Devotion of Suspect X (2008)#

This movie shines a light at Yukawa’s character, as he has to grapple with the fact that one of the few people he considers a genuine genius and a kindred spirit (The one I mentioned in the previous movie) has thrown it all for reasons he can’t understand.

Despite seeing the crime being committed at the start of the movie, it manages to twist it at the end for an even bigger emotional impact.

On the other hand, Utsumi and the MPD take a more background role, mainly supporting Yukawa while following their own lead. You still get to see plenty of interaction between them, while also seeing the trust Yukawa now has in Utsumi.

Season 2 (2013)#

Season 2 is set at the same time as its release year, 6 years after the original series. Most of the background cast has been changed, and while Utsumi’s actress remains the same, she very quickly takes a background role as she leaves for a different job in the States. Thankfully, detective Kishitani Misa (Yoshitaka Yuriko) joins the cast as a her replacement.

Kishitani’s personality is completely different from Utsumi’s, being more of a prideful girl who knows she’s good (and pretty) and is initially insulted she has to deal with a weirdo like Yukawa, but just like her predecesor, she eventually grows fond of him.

We also get some confirmation of the friendship between Yukawa and Utsumi in the way of the puzzles he receives from her and, according to his students, always looks forward to solving.

Overall the season does what S1 did just as well, with new interesting cases and science behind them, as well as some more development between the characters.

Movie 2 - Galileo XX: Utsumi Kaoru’s Last Case (2013)#

This movie is set between seasons 1 and 2, and reveals more of the reason why Utsumi decides to go to the US. Initially being pressured into doing it by her superior, after the events of the story decides to go of her own free will for the experience.

Following the theme of the movies, this one focuses on, and highlights the, abilities of Utsumi as a detective, showing how capable she is in a male dominated field, despite her own lack of self confidence, while leaving Yukawa more in the background as support when needed.

This is something all movies do that I love. They all focus more on one character (or the relationship between two or more of them) and lets them shine.

Movie 3 - Midsummer’s Equation (2013)#

Finding this movie took me the most effort, almost having to resort to an italian hardsub, but thankfully managed to find a clean 1080p torrent for it.

This movie once again focuses on Yukawa. This time being about how he, despite his hatred for children due to their irrationality (and the rashes he gets), manages to get attached to the one whose family is the central case of this movie, thanks to that kid being willing to learn about science for his summer homework.

It’s a nice look into how Yukawa is willing to share his knowledge if the other person wants to learn, and how sometimes he’s willing to not be fully cold when solving a case if it’s for the greater good.

Movie 4 - Silent Parade (2022)#

The second to last movie released, and one done years after season 2’s original run.

While this movie focuses both on Yukawa and detective Kusanagi solving a crime involving a small town, the one who truly shines here is Kusanagi himself, both from a character arc view, with him learning to deal with the consequences of a past case he was not able to fully close and what has happened now due to it, as well as from an acting perspective, as Kitamura Kazuki absolutely killed it with the emotional scenes.

Movie 5 - Forbidden Magic (2022)#

Sadly, the last movie released so far.

Forbidden Magic shows the special considerations Yukawa has towards people he considers geniuses, as he wouldn’t go to such lengths for almost any other culprit, similar to the first movie.

What this movie does in particular, though, is highlight the trust Yukawa and Kusanagi have for each other, with the climax being an implicit “I know you’ll do what’s right” from Yukawa, with Kusanagi calling him his best friend.

Closing Words#

Out of everything I have played, watched, read, etc, this year, this has got to be among my favorite. Even now, I feel somewhat empty after having finished it and not having any more content to consume.

Sure, I could read the novels, but they are fundamentally different as Kusanagi is the main detective, plus the fact that they are lacking the music I came to like and the actors I’ve come to enjoy watching. Even when trying to find other series to watch, all I keep thinking is “Damn, this isn’t as good as Galileo was”, and I think a big part of it is JDramas often being 10-12 episodes long one-and-done deals, while Galileo has 2 seasons and 6 movies/specials to really get you into it.
